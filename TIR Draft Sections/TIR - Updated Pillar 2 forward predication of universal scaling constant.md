## Section II: The Level-Invariant Boundary Proof

A critical requirement for any hierarchical or scale-invariant framework is the non-circular verification of its scaling parameters. If the scaling factor ($\Omega$) between adjacent levels must be inferred by cross-contaminating data across those levels, the model risks becoming a mathematical artifact rather than a physical reality.

To ensure epistemological rigor, this section demonstrates that the absolute structural scale of the sub-quantum substrate floor (Level $N-1$) can be treated as a pure independent output, derived strictly from empirical observations accessible entirely within our native atomic domain (Level $N$).

### 2.1 The Relational Field Ratio

An observer restricted to Level $N$ can measure two distinct, orthogonal expressions of a stable fundamental soliton (the proton): its radial displacement gradient (expressed as electrostatic charge, $F\_e$) and its tangential wake footprint (expressed as gravity, $F\_g$).

When measured between two fundamental fermions at a fixed distance, modern metrology yields a precise, dimensionless ratio:

$$\frac{F\_e}{F\_g} \approx 1.23 \times 10^{36}$$

Within the kinematic framework, these forces are not distinct, independent fields. They represent the localized radial pumping velocity ($v\_{\text{rad}}$) and macro-scale tangential circulation ($v\_{\text{tan}}$) of a single fluid medium. Because the medium's kinetic energy dilutes volumetrically as it transitions from a high-stiffness micro-puncture to a macro-scale spatial ripple, this field attenuation scales as a squared geometric function of the boundary layer propagation across the universal octave.

Therefore, the dimensionless force ratio is the direct physical expression of the Harmonic Scaling Factor squared ($\Omega^2$):

$$\Omega^2 = \frac{F\_e}{F\_g}$$

Taking the geometric square root of this purely local data isolates the exact, unvalued scaling constant governing the universal gear-shift:

$$\Omega = \sqrt{\frac{F\_e}{F\_g}} \approx 5.20 \times 10^{19}$$

### 2.2 Deductive Derivation of the Substrate Floor

Having isolated $\Omega$ using exclusively Level $N$ forces, the observer can now calculate the absolute physical dimensions of the sub-quantum basement (Level $N-1$) without ever measuring it directly.

By applying the linear scaling rule to the empirically verified CODATA charge radius of the proton ($r\_{\text{proton}} \approx 8.41 \times 10^{-16}\ \text{m}$), the native structural node of the underlying substrate ($r\_{\text{substrate}}$) emerges mechanically:

$$r\_{\text{substrate}} = \frac{r\_{\text{proton}}}{\Omega}$$

$$r\_{\text{substrate}} = \frac{8.41 \times 10^{-16}\ \text{m}}{5.20 \times 10^{19}} = 1.61 \times 10^{-35}\ \text{m}$$

This output maps with absolute mathematical identity to the standard **Planck length** ($\ell\_P$). The fact that the boundary of the sub-quantum floor can be derived directly from the macro-scale force balance of an atomic particle validates the structural rigidity of the framework. The photon and the proton act as real-time, local records of the basement's physical dimensions.

### 2.3 Metrological Limits and the Gravitational Bottleneck

It is vital to address the precision boundaries of this derivation. The calculation does not introduce loose, sliding, or fine-tuned parameters. Instead, its observational accuracy is strictly bound by the limitations of modern experimental metrology.

$$\Omega = \sqrt{\frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon\_0 G m\_p^2}}$$

While the elementary charge ($e$), vacuum permittivity ($\epsilon\_0$), and proton mass ($m\_p$) are known to extreme sub-part-per-billion precision, Newton’s gravitational constant ($G$) remains constrained by the mechanical limitations of macroscopic torsion balance experiments, carrying an inherent relative uncertainty of approximately 22 parts per million.

Because $G$ dominates the error budget inside the square root, the precision of our derived scale factor tracks the exact experimental error boundaries of the standard Planck scale calculations. The framework introduces no new mathematical noise; it simply reinterprets existing empirical constants through a single, continuous kinematic primitive ($v^2 = a \cdot r$), providing a significantly more direct and integrated picture of the spatial hierarchy.
