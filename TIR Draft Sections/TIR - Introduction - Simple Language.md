1. **Radius ($r$):** The physical size, boundary, or distance of a fluid disruption.

3. **Radial Velocity ($v_{\text{rad}}$):** The *pulsing speed* of the fluid moving directly inward or outward from a central point.

## How Motion Constructs the "Nouns" of Physics

Using these four simple tools, the abstract properties of modern physics reveal themselves as straightforward mechanical descriptions of fluid behavior.

### 1. Mass is Trapped Spinning Speed

When fluid spins around a tiny coordinate, it creates a localized gyroscope. In high school physics, we know that spinning objects resist being moved linearly—this is called rotational inertia. What we measure on our laboratory scales as "solid mass" ($M$) is simply the spatial torque of this spinning fluid loop:

$$M = v_{\text{tan}}^2 \cdot r$$

### 2. Charge is Pulsing Speed

Electric charge ($Q$) is not a magical fluid poured into a particle. It is the measurement of the fluid fabric breathing or vibrating in and out from a central core. Positive and negative charges are simply phase-locked pulses moving inward or outward. Charge is the radial flux of the continuum:

$$Q = v_{\text{rad}} \cdot r$$

This cleanly explains why mass and charge behave differently: they are simply two different directional movements of the exact same fluid substrate. Spin the fluid, and you create the inertia of mass; pulse the fluid, and you create the fields of charge.
