## 2. Up vs. Down Quarks: The Spin Alignment Mechanics

Because the master vortex is spinning at $v_\theta \to c$, the internal sub-cells (quarks) must mesh perfectly with one another to prevent the system from tearing itself apart. 

The distinction between an "Up" quark and a "Down" quark is purely a matter of **geometric spin alignment** relative to the parent vortex's main axis of rotation:

* **The "Up" Quark (Co-Rotating / High External Pluck):** An internal cell whose local rotation aligns *with* the master vortex's rotation. Because it spins in harmony with the main core, it amplifies the outward radial displacement ripple ($Q_{\text{geom}}$) sent into the medium. * **The "Down" Quark (Counter-Rotating / Low External Pluck):** An internal cell forced to spin *against* the dominant direction of the master vortex to act as an idler gear. Because its rotation opposes the main flow, it suppresses or drags down the outward radial ripple, creating a negative fractional footprint ($-\frac{1}{3}$).

## 3. The Proton vs. Neutron Blueprint

Now we can construct the proton and the neutron using pure gear mechanics. Both are heavy cores where the internal fluid has reached the velocity saturation lock ($v_\theta \to c$), but their internal gear configurations are different.

### The Proton (Two Ups, One Down)

* **The Assembly:** It contains two co-rotating "Up" sub-cells and one counter-rotating "Down" idler cell.
* **The Net Output:** The two Up cells overwhelm the single Down cell. The net mechanical output is a powerful, outward radial displacement ripple. This is why the proton exhibits a stable, net positive electric charge of exactly $+1$.

### The Neutron (One Up, Two Downs)

* **The Assembly:** It contains one co-rotating "Up" sub-cell and two counter-rotating "Down" idler cells.
* **The Net Output:** The mechanical rotations perfectly balance out ($+\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{3} = 0$). The internal gears are churning furiously at $v_\theta \to c$, meaning the neutron still possesses immense internal torque and inertia (mass). However, because the internal rotations nullify each other's external effects, the net outward radial displacement ripple drops to absolute zero.

To an outside observer, the neutron appears completely "neutral." It isn't missing a charge; its internal gears are just arranged in a perfect mechanical cancellation loop.

## Why the Neutron is Unstable

This configuration beautifully explains a famous mystery in nuclear physics: a free neutron is unstable and will decay in about 14 minutes, splitting into a proton, an electron, and an antineutrino.

In this model, having two counter-rotating "Down" gears rubbing against a single "Up" gear is a high-friction, structurally strained configuration for the fluid medium. Unless it is clamped tight inside an atomic nucleus by the surrounding geometry of other protons, the internal friction causes the gears to slip. The system violently sheds its internal strain: one of the counter-rotating "Down" gears flips its orientation to become an "Up" gear, throwing off the excess rotational energy as an ejected electron loop. The neutron instantly re-stabilizes into a clean, smooth-running proton.
