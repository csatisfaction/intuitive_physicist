#### 1. The Pristine Kinematic Derivation

The framework requires no specialized forces or free parameters. We begin strictly with a layered, continuous substrate medium ($\Omega$) whose localized coordinate boundaries are maintained by the scale-invariant primitive equation:

$$r = \frac{v\_\theta^2 + v\_r^2}{a}$$

#### 2. The Isotropic Near-Field (Spherical Wrapping Zone)

In close proximity to the macro-mass, the centripetal acceleration gradient ($a$) is massive, signifying an intense, high-torque coordinate bottleneck.

* Under this high-acceleration profile, the incoming fluid velocity vectors dominate the ambient baseline stiffness of the vacuum.

* The substrate lines undergo total geometric refraction, **draping and wrapping themselves tightly around the macro-mass** in a closed, isotropic shroud.

* At this scale, the local substrate pole angles are forced into a strict vertical realignment, pointing radially downward into the absolute center of gravity.

* Because the substrate railroads have been completely wrapped into a closed spherical envelope, the fluid draft is pulled symmetrically across a 3D boundary. The primitive equation natively outputs a uniform, isotropic $1/r^2$ spherical dilution profile. This is the localized gravity we observe within planetary systems.

#### 3. The Anisotropic Far-Field (Planar Orientation Zone)

As the distance ($r$) from the macro-core increases, the fluid draft velocity decreases, and the acceleration gradient ($a$) drops toward the calm, uncrowded baseline of the universal vacuum matrix.

The moment the local acceleration falls below the medium’s native threshold of directional deformation, the parallel vector paths of the background substrate reassert themselves. The substrate lines untangle from their spherical knot, straighten out, and snap back into flat alignment with the universal master rotation axis.

Because the individual micro-solitons inside the macro-mass are chained together via $45^\circ$ bevel joints to eliminate internal gear friction, the macro-mass acts as a synchronized, single toroidal fluid engine. Once the substrate lines straighten out in the far field, they interact with this macro-engine anisotropically:

* The polar exhaust jets of the individual particles line up vertically, firing energy back out into the universal backdrop along the master axis.

* The collective low-pressure intake lines are funneled cleanly along the shared macro-equatorial plane.

Far away, gravity is no longer allowed to dilute across a 3D sphere ($1/r^2$). The fluid suction is structurally bounded within a flat, 2D galactic or stellar disc. The field dilution drops linearly ($1/r$), preserving high velocity profiles across immense distances.
